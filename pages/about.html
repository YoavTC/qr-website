More About Me

My passion for creating experiences that I and others could enjoy started at a very young age. I made a board game with my mother about gnomes stealing gems from a hut. My mom made the art and the pieces because she's an artist, and I was a literal child.

I don't really remember the rules of the game, but I remember enjoying playing it with her and, when it was finished, playing with friends.



I was never a good student. I was a troublemaker, hated listening to teachers, and participated in stupid group activities.I spent most of my time at school daydreaming and coming up with new ideas.

Of course, I also hated doing homework, so I played video games instead. The first game I ever played was 'Mogobe'. A social club penguin-like kind of game where you have to collect dragon pets, raise them, and decorate your house. The game also had an in-game currency, and you could get more money by playing mini-games found in certain places.

Although I almost never play those games, my dream game is to create an MMO RPG that has a limited world to explore but tons of quests and combat with other players or NPCs.

I got pretty close to it when I created my Minecraft Server utilizing the already existing game mechanics and easy addon support.

If you want to reach me, feel free to visit the Contact page (:



A bit on how I started creating games:

I started learning how to code when I was 13 years old using Skript language for my Minecraft server, and at that time, my high school introduced a new computer science class in C#, which I was happy to join.

Unfortunately, I had to drop out because I was in the middle of transferring to a new school, but, using the knowledge I gained, I started developing games in Unity, and created my first-ever game, Chef Shooter.

While I enjoyed coding the game, I wanted to bring my passion for Minecraft into my projects, so, I began modding Minecraft using the Fabric Library.

Soon I began to create some mods that I felt fit the style of the vanilla game, you can check out all of my projects on the Projects page of my website.
